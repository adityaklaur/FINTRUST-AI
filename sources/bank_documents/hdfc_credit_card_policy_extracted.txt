## Policy for Issuance and Conduct of Credit Cards

Background:

HDFC Bank Limited (“Bank”) as part of its operations issues Credit Cards (as hereinafter defined) and is a member of various card networks for issuance of Credit Cards including Visa, MasterCard, Diners and Rupay. The Bank also enters into co-branding arrangements, from to time, which contemplate the issuance of Co-Branded Credit Cards (as hereinafter defined). The Bank started the credit cards business in November 2001.

Today, the Bank is a leading player in terms of issuance, portfolio size and quality and it has leveraged the existing HDFC Bank liability and asset customers for sourcing credit cards and also through open market sourcing including arrangements under Co-branded Credit Cards. Business growth, profitability and asset quality have been the cornerstones of its strategy across all the card products. Bank currently issues credit card under individual credit card, corporate credit card and business credit card (including charge cards).

The issuance and conduct of credit card by the Bank is governed by the Regulatory Guidelines issued by RBI. In terms of the RBI’s master directions titled ‘Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions’ dated November 28, 2025 amended from time to time, each card-issuer is required to have a well-documented Board approved policy for the issuance and conduct of Credit Cards

This comprehensive Policy aims to document the framework for issuance, conduct of Credit Card operations by the Bank, including Co-Branded Credit Cards, in consonance with the directions of the RBI from time to time [and good industry practices and procedures]. The policy also covers aspects related to the variants issued by the Bank underwriting standards that are internal to the Bank as a part of comprehensive guidance to the various in the Bank. Other regulations like NPA norms governed under various circulars of RBI are also applicable.

The Credit Card Business is conducted in compliance with the extant Regulatory guidelines and the Bank is committed to follow the prescribed standards of banking practices as detailed in the “Code of Bank’s Commitment” to customers.

Card Product Details:

The credit card variants issued by the Bank shall be within the four types of Credit Cards permitted to be issued by the Bank notified Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions’ dated November 28, 2025 updated from time to time:

(a) Credit Cards issued to individuals; 
(b) Business Credit Cards to entities including Charge cards. 
(c) Corporate Credit Cards; and 
(d) Co-branded Credit Cards. 

The Bank may issue business credit cards to business entities / individuals for business expenses. The business credit cards may also be issued as charge cards, corporate credit cards or by linking a credit facility such as overdraft / cash credit provided for business purpose as per the terms and conditions stipulated for the facility concerned.

It also offers credit cards through various form factors such as Physical credit cards(Plastic and Metal) and Virtual Credit Cards to customers subject to certain criteria. Credit Card sourcing is in line with the extant regulatory guidelines.

The bank provides the following credit card variants: Classic, Premium & Co-Brand and Super Premium Cards. All cards carry an accelerated Rewards/Cashback on spends, activation & milestone benefits and other card offerings towards lounge access & fuel-surcharge waiver as decided from time to time based on Marketing proposition of the Bank.

The bank provides the “Corporate Credit Card(s)” issued to specific employee/s of a corporate employer wherein the liability could rest with the corporate entity or the employee or jointly on both, as per the product design features”.

On issuing corporate credit cards, the responsibilities and liabilities of the corporate and its employees are clearly specified. The liability of the corporate/business entity shall form part of its assessed credits.

Business Cards issued to corporates (Purchase cards) allow designated employees/ officers to pay their supplier (for goods and services) centrally using the card.

All HDFC Bank Credit Cards issued are Contactless Cards which facilitate fast, convenient & secure payments. Contactless cards enablement is basis customer consent. HDFC Bank Credit Cards support Tokenization which is replacement of the cardholder’s Primary Account Number (PAN) with an alternate number, referred to as a token. Token number is a 16-digit unique number assigned by the franchiser/bank basis the active BIN ranges shared by the Bank.

All HDFC Bank credit card members can avail of up to 3 add-on cards where the limit on the primary credit card is shared with the add-on card members. Bank shall block a lost card immediately on being informed by the cardholder, card members are covered for lost card liability i.e., once a card member reports loss of card to HDFC Bank within 48 hours, the liability for any subsequent transaction on that card is not on the card member. Lost Card liability is applicable for all card variants.

Issuance of Credit Cards:

(a) The Bank will convey all information about its processes and procedures to maintain 
transparency as part of both the digital and physical journeys at the time of issuance of a credit 
card. 
(b) The Bank shall obtain all necessary consents required from the customer at the time of 
customer onboarding in terms of the RBI Directions (including as may be required for issuance 
of credit card, activation of credit card within 30 days as specified by RBI post issuance of the 
card, sharing of customer data, availing of insurance linked to the Credit Cards) in a fair and 
transparent manner as permitted by RBI from time to time. Any digital modes introduced for 
seeking consent for issuance of card or other products/services offered along with the card 
shall be reported to the Department of Regulation, Reserve Bank of India. 
(c) The terms and conditions applicable to the Credit Cards including, without limitation, the most 
important terms and conditions (“MITC”) and Key Fact Statement (KFS) shall be 
communicated to the customer in a transparent manner and a copy of the MITC and 
agreement shall be provided to the cardholder as required under the ‘Reserve Bank of India 
(Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions’ dated 
November 28, 2025 . 
(d) In case of rejection of a Credit Card application, the Bank shall convey the same along with the 
reason/s which led to the rejection of the application, in writing in the form of physical letter 
or through any other electronic form. 
(e) The decision-making power for issue of Credit Card to a customer shall remain only with the 
Bank. The credit limit is arrived basis multipliers after factoring in the customer type (salaried 
/ self-employed), monthly income etc. The final credit limit is assigned after factoring in the 
estimated loan and credit card obligations from the credit bureau record of the customer. 
(f) The Bank provides option at the time of issuance as well as renewal to the customer to choose 
amongst the eligible Credit Card variants with specific payment network partners including 
Visa, Master Card, RuPay, Diners Cub (DGN) or any other partner that bank would sign-up in 
future. This allows flexibility to bank as well as customers to choose the best of the offering 
conducive to each segment of customers.

#### Reporting to Credit Information Companies

The Bank shall comply with all reporting requirements under the Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Information Reporting) Directions, 2025 including with respect to reporting of any information to the credit information companies. In the matter of recovery of dues, the Bank shall ensure that it, as also its agents, adhere to the extant instructions on Fair Practices Code and RBI Directions.

Card Closure

Any request for closure of a Credit Card shall be honoured by the Bank within 7 working days from the date of receiving the request or as may be prescribed under applicable guidelines from time to time, subject to payment of all dues by the cardholder.

In case payment towards dues is outstanding, the card-issuer shall provide details regarding such outstanding dues to the cardholder upon receipt of the closure request without waiting for completion of the billing cycle and accordingly advise him/her to make payment in order to complete the closure process. In such cases, the prescribed timeline of seven working days shall be calculated after excluding the number of days taken by the cardholder to clear the outstanding dues

Customer can activate their card via various modes by performing their first retail transaction via E Com, POS, UPI or setting card pin via MyCards/ PayZapp, Net Banking, Mobile Banking, IVR, ATM. The Bank shall close all inactivated cards within 30 days of issuance of card with a 7 working day intimation period and all such inactivated cards post issuance shall not be reported to bureau. Any

credit card that is not activated by the card holder for more than 30 days from the date of issuance can only be activated with One Time Password (OTP) based consent modes (between 31-37 days).

Similarly, all cards not used for a period of more than one year shall be closed with 30 days advance intimation to the customer, subject to payment of all dues to the Bank. The information regarding the closure of card account shall also accordingly be updated with the Credit Information Company/ies within a period of 30 days.

Credit Card Billing Cycle:

The Bank shall ensure that the customer is provided with sufficient number of days in compliance with applicable law for making payment of any bill in relation to the Credit Cards.

The Bank provides options to the cardholders to modify the billing cycle of the Credit Card at least once, as per their convenience, by reaching out through phone banking, email or by sending a physical letter. The list of channels is updated in billing statement and MITC.

Compensation Framework:

Bank had issued a credit card without written/ digital consent of the customer and if the card is activated by the Bank without the cardholder’s consent (intent for use by cardholder shall also be considered as cardholder’s consent), the Bank would reverse the charges levied by the Bank for the card variant immediately and also pay a penalty without demur to the customer amounting to twice the value of charges reversed in this regard.

Failure on the part of the card-issuers to complete the process of closure within seven working days shall result in a penalty of ₹500 per day of delay payable to the customer, till the closure of the credit card provided there is no outstanding in the Credit card account.

Definitions:

o “Business Credit Card(s)” shall mean the Credit Card(s) that is issued to business entities/individuals for the purpose of business expenses with specified terms and conditions and not for personal use;

o “Co-Branded Credit Card(s)” shall mean the Credit Card(s) that is issued jointly by the Bank and a co-branding entity bearing the names of both the partnering entities;

o “Corporate Credit Card(s)” shall mean the Credit Card(s) that is issued to specific employee/s of a corporate employer wherein the liability could rest with the corporate entity or the employee or jointly on both, as per the product design features;

o “Credit Card(s)” shall mean a physical or virtual payment instrument(s) containing a means of identification, issued with a pre-approved revolving credit limit, that can be used to purchase goods and services or draw cash advances, subject to prescribed terms and conditions;

o “Add-On Credit Card(s)” shall a supplementary credit card to the principal or primary credit card, issued to person/s with predefined relationship with the primary cardholder, within the credit limits and liability of the latter, subject to such terms and conditions as stipulated by the card-issuer;

o “Policy” shall mean this "Credit Cards- Issuance and Conduct Policy and Product Program" together with all schedules and annexures hereto, as the same may be amended, modified,

restated or substituted from time to time in accordance with the terms hereof;

o “Billing Cycle” shall mean the regular length of time between closing dates of two consecutive bills raised by the card-issuer;

o “Interest Free Credit period” shall the time period from the date of transaction to the due date of payment, wherein interest free payment can be made, subject to the payment of entire outstanding on or before the payment due date by the cardholder;

o “Minimum Amount Due” shall mean the minimum amount of money, as a part of the total bill amount, that a cardholder has to pay to not be treated as an overdue bill;

o “Total Amount Due” shall the total amount (net of credit received during the billing cycle, if any) payable by the cardholder as per the credit card statement generated at the end of a billing cycle;

o “RBI” shall mean the Reserve Bank of India;

o “RBI Directions” shall mean all applicable directions and guidelines issued by the RBI in relation to the issuance and conduct of credit cards including RBI’s master directions titled ‘Reserve Bank of India (Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions’ dated November 28, 2025 as amended from time to time.

o “Charge Card” shall mean the credit card where the user has to pay the billed amount in full on due date after the billing cycle, and no rolling over of credit to the next billing cycle is permitted.
