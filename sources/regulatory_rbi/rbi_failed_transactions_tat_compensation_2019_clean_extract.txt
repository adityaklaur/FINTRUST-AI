Title: Harmonisation of Turn Around Time (TAT) and customer compensation for failed transactions using authorised Payment Systems
Source URL: https://www.rbi.org.in/commonperson/English/Scripts/Notification.aspx?Id=3074
Date: 20/09/2019
RBI reference: RBI/2019-20/67 DPSS.CO.PD No.629/02.01.014/2019-20
Source type: RBI notification clean text extract

Purpose for FinTrust AI:
Use this source for failed transaction questions, UPI/IMPS/card/ATM reversal timelines, and compensation logic. This is one of the most important sources for the "money debited but not credited" workflow.

Key regulatory text:

The Reserve Bank put in place a framework on Turn Around Time (TAT) for resolution of customer complaints and compensation across authorised payment systems.

A failed transaction is a transaction which has not been fully completed due to a reason not attributable to the customer, such as failure in communication links, non-availability of cash in an ATM, time-out of sessions, non-credit to beneficiary account, or delay in initiating a reversal transaction.

Wherever financial compensation is involved, it shall be effected to the customer's account suo moto, without waiting for a complaint or claim from the customer.

Customers who do not get the benefit of redress as defined in the TAT can register a complaint with the Reserve Bank Integrated Ombudsman Scheme.

Important TAT and compensation table:

1. ATM / Micro-ATM
- Incident: Customer account debited but cash not dispensed.
- Timeline: Proactive reversal within maximum T + 5 days.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day of delay beyond T + 5 days.

2. Card to card transfer
- Incident: Card account debited but beneficiary card account not credited.
- Timeline: Transaction reversed latest within T + 1 day if credit is not effected.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day of delay beyond T + 1 day.

3. POS / card present including cash at POS
- Incident: Account debited but confirmation not received at merchant location / charge slip not generated.
- Timeline: Auto-reversal within T + 5 days.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day of delay beyond T + 5 days.

4. Card not present / e-commerce
- Incident: Account debited but confirmation not received at merchant system.
- Timeline: Auto-reversal within T + 5 days.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day of delay beyond T + 5 days.

5. IMPS
- Incident: Account debited but beneficiary account not credited.
- Timeline: If unable to credit beneficiary account, auto reversal by beneficiary bank latest on T + 1 day.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 1 day.

6. UPI fund transfer
- Incident: Account debited but beneficiary account not credited.
- Timeline: If unable to credit beneficiary account, auto reversal by beneficiary bank latest on T + 1 day.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 1 day.

7. UPI merchant payment
- Incident: Account debited but transaction confirmation not received at merchant location.
- Timeline: Auto-reversal within T + 5 days.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 5 days.

8. Aadhaar Enabled Payment System
- Incident: Account debited but transaction confirmation not received at merchant location, or beneficiary account not credited.
- Timeline: Acquirer to initiate credit adjustment within T + 5 days.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 5 days.

9. APBS
- Incident: Delay in crediting beneficiary account.
- Timeline: Beneficiary bank to reverse the transaction within T + 1 day.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 1 day.

10. NACH
- Incident: Delay in crediting beneficiary account or reversal of amount.
- Timeline: Beneficiary bank to reverse uncredited transaction within T + 1 day.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 1 day.

11. PPI / wallet on-us transaction
- Incident: Beneficiary PPI not credited or PPI debited but transaction confirmation not received at merchant location.
- Timeline: Reversal effected in remitter account within T + 1 day.
- Compensation: Rs. 100 per day if delay is beyond T + 1 day.

Implementation notes:
- This source should be used as primary grounding for failed transaction TAT answers.
- The assistant should distinguish P2P UPI transfer from UPI merchant payment.
- The assistant should not promise compensation in every case; it should state conditions and cite the source.
- The assistant should ask for transaction date, amount, channel, UTR/reference ID, app/bank, and complaint reference number.
