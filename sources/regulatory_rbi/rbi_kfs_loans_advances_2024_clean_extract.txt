Title: Key Facts Statement (KFS) for Loans and Advances
Source URL: https://rbi.org.in/Scripts/NotificationUser.aspx?Id=12663&Mode=0
Date: 15/04/2024
RBI reference: RBI/2024-25/18 DOR.STR.REC.13/13.03.00/2024-25
Source type: RBI notification clean text extract

Purpose for FinTrust AI:
Use this for loan transparency complaints, hidden charges, APR disclosure, KFS validity, undisclosed fees, digital lending cooling-off period, and retail/MSME term loan user journeys.

Key points:

- The KFS is a statement of key facts of a loan agreement in simple and easier-to-understand language.
- It must be provided to prospective borrowers to help them make an informed decision before executing the loan contract.
- The KFS must be written in a language understood by the borrower.
- Contents of the KFS must be explained to the borrower and acknowledgement obtained.
- The KFS must have a unique proposal number.
- The KFS must have validity of at least three working days for loans with tenor of seven days or more.
- For loans with tenor less than seven days, validity must be at least one working day.
- The KFS must include APR computation and amortisation schedule over the loan tenor.
- APR includes interest rate and all charges associated with the credit facility.
- Charges recovered from borrowers by REs on behalf of third-party service providers, such as insurance or legal charges, form part of APR and must be disclosed separately.
- If the RE is involved in recovering third-party charges, receipts and related documents must be provided to the borrower for each payment within a reasonable time.
- Any fees or charges not mentioned in the KFS cannot be charged to the borrower during the loan term without explicit consent.
- The KFS must be included as a summary box in the loan agreement.
- Credit card receivables are exempted from this circular.
- These instructions apply to new retail and MSME term loans sanctioned on or after October 1, 2024.

Implementation notes:

- Add classifier category: `loan_kfs_or_apr_disclosure`.
- Ask for KFS copy, loan agreement, sanction letter, fee name, fee amount, and whether borrower gave explicit consent.
- This is highly relevant for digital loans, personal loans, MSME term loans and home loan fee complaints.
- Do not apply this circular to credit card receivables.
