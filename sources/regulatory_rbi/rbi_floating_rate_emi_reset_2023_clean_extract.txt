Title: Reset of Floating Interest Rate on EMI-based Personal Loans
Source URL: https://www.rbi.org.in/Scripts/NotificationUser.aspx?Id=12529
Date: 18/08/2023, updated 01/10/2025
RBI reference: RBI/2023-24/55 DOR.MCS.REC.32/01.01.003/2023-24
Source type: RBI notification clean text extract

Purpose for FinTrust AI:
Use this for floating-rate personal loan / home loan complaints where borrowers say EMI or tenor changed without clear communication or consent.

Key points:

- At sanction of EMI-based floating-rate personal loans, regulated entities must consider repayment capacity and possible future increase in benchmark rate.
- RBI noted grievances about elongation of loan tenor and/or increase in EMI without proper communication or consent.
- At the time of sanction, REs must clearly communicate the possible impact of benchmark-rate changes on EMI, tenor, or both.
- Any increase in EMI or tenor due to rate reset must be communicated immediately through appropriate channels.
- At the time of interest-rate reset, REs may provide a choice to switch to fixed rate as per board-approved policy.
- Borrowers must be given choices to:
  - increase EMI,
  - extend tenor,
  - use a combination of EMI increase and tenor extension,
  - prepay in part or full at any point during the tenor.
- Foreclosure charges / prepayment penalty are subject to extant RBI instructions.
- Charges for switching from floating to fixed rate and related administrative costs must be transparently disclosed in the sanction letter and when revised.
- Tenor extension in floating-rate loans must not result in negative amortisation.
- REs must provide or make accessible quarterly statements showing principal and interest recovered, EMI amount, number of EMIs left, and annualized rate of interest / APR.

Implementation notes:

- Add classifier category: `floating_rate_emi_reset`.
- Ask user for loan type, bank/NBFC, sanction date, current EMI, original EMI, tenure change, communication received, and whether KFS/loan agreement disclosed reset impact.
- Do not tell user that fixed-rate switch is always mandatory. Wording says REs may provide that choice as per board-approved policy.
- Use this source with KFS/APR and interest-rate-on-advances sources.
